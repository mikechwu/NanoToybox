What we collect

atomdojo is a browser-based molecular-simulation playground. Most usage — exploring in Lab, watching in Watch, downloading local capsules — never contacts our servers and never stores anything about you. Signing in and publishing public share links do, and the table below is the exhaustive list of what we collect when you do.

Service providers we use

  • Cloudflare, Inc. — hosting (Pages), API execution (Pages Functions), database (D1), object storage (R2), and edge security (WAF). All data described above is stored on Cloudflare infrastructure. Policy: cloudflare.com/privacypolicy.
  • Google LLC — OAuth 2.0 sign-in via "Continue with Google". When you choose this path, Google receives your browser's request to grant us your email address, display name, and a stable account identifier. Policy: policies.google.com/privacy.
  • GitHub, Inc. (Microsoft) — OAuth 2.0 sign-in via "Continue with GitHub". Same pattern as Google. Policy: GitHub privacy statement.

Public share links

Anyone with the share code or link can open a published capsule in Watch without signing in, until the owner deletes it. Do not publish anything you would not want a stranger to be able to view.

Affirmative statements

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

Children under 13

This service is general-audience and not intended for children under 13. We ask you to confirm you are at least 13 each time you sign in, and the sign-in flow is enforced server-side: signing in is not possible without that confirmation, and publishing is not possible without an acceptance record on file. We do not knowingly collect personal information from users under 13.

If you believe an account was created by a child under 13, contact us through the privacy contact channel below and we will delete the account and associated data within 14 days.

Deleting a single share

From Account → Uploads you can delete any capsule you have published. When you do:

  • The share record is tombstoned immediately — the row keeps its share code, owner ID, and aggregate metrics (size, atom count, frame count) for audit integrity, but its status flips to deleted.
  • Content-identifying fields (sha256, preview poster + motion keys) are nulled.
  • The R2 blob is removed.
  • The public share link starts returning 404 on the next request — anyone who already had the link or code loses access.

Deleting your account

From Account → Privacy & Data you can delete every uploaded capsule in one action, or delete your account entirely. Account deletion runs an authoritative cascade:

  1. All active sessions are revoked.
  2. Per-user publish quota records are removed.
  3. Every capsule you own is tombstoned via the same flow as single-share deletion (R2 blobs removed, public links return 404).
  4. OAuth account links are deleted.
  5. Your users row is tombstoned: display name nulled, a deletion timestamp recorded. The row itself is retained as a tombstone so audit references (actor, owner_user_id) still resolve.
  6. A single account-deletion audit event is recorded with the per-capsule outcome summary.

After your account is deleted, signing in again with the same provider creates a brand-new account with a new identity. No prior content is restored.

Your rights and our response timelines

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access the personal information we hold, request its deletion (GDPR Art. 17; CCPA right to delete), object to or restrict processing, and request portability where technically feasible. The in-product flows above cover access (the Account page) and deletion. For anything else, use the privacy contact channel below. We will respond within 30 days (EEA / UK requests) or 45 days (California requests), with one extension where the request is complex (up to 90 days maximum in either jurisdiction).

Audit retention

We keep an append-only audit log of operationally significant events (publishes, deletions, abuse reports, sweeper runs). The only PII-adjacent fields on those records are a hashed IP and a truncated User-Agent string.

A weekly cron job scrubs those two fields on every audit row older than 180 days, and row-deletes abuse-report rows past the same threshold. The event skeleton (event type, share code, timestamp, severity) is retained indefinitely for operational forensics — that skeleton contains no personal information once the IP hash and User-Agent are nulled.
